Case Study 7: Gillian

Gillian is a 25 year old woman, gravida 10000, at 41 1/7weeks gestation. She presented to L&D for a scheduled non-stress test to asses fetal well-being.

Gillian's medical history is unremarkable; surgery history includes internal fixation of a broken arm after a fall from monkey bars at age 7. Gillian's pre-pregnancy BMI was 20, and she gained 40 pounds during her pregnancy, more than the Institute of Medicine's recommended weight gain of 25–35 pounds for a normal weight woman with a singleton pregnancy. The first trimester ultrasound was consistent with Gillian's last menstrual period and second trimester screenings were normal.

Gillian's vaginal examination (VE) during her last prenatal visit at 40 3/7 weeks gestation was 2 cm dilated, 50% effaced, and the fetal head at –2 station. Her vital signs this morning are as follows: temperature oral, 98.6° F (37° C); blood pressure, 110/72 mmHg; pulse, 72; and, respirations, 18 breaths per minute. Gillian states that she is experiencing contractions about 10 to 20 minutes apart that are 3 on a pain scale of 10. After explaining how a non-stress test is performed and placing Gillian in left lateral position, the nurse applied the U/S and tocodynamometer and started the non-stress test.

Identify the FHR characteristics evident in Gillian's non-stress fetal tracing:

1) What is the baseline fetal heart rate?

Answer: 135 - Approximate mean FHR rounded to increments of 5 bpm during a 10-minute window excluding accelerations and decelerations and periods of marked variability. There must be ≥ 2 minutes of identifiable baseline segments.

2) What is the variability?

Answer: Moderate - Amplitude range (6-25 bmp).

3) Are accelerations present?

Answer: Yes - This non-stress test is reactive.

4) What is the frequency of the contractions?

Answer: None - Contraction frequency is measured from the beginning of one contraction to the beginning of the next and is expressed in minutes.

The nurse called the physician to report the NST was completed and was reactive. She also told the physician that Gillian thinks she might be in early labor, although no contractions occurred during the non-stress test. The physician reviewed the non-stress test remotely and requested the nurse perform a VE. Gillian's VE revealed her cervix 3 cm dilated, 100% effaced, and a fetal vertex at –1. The physician asked the nurse to discharge Gillian after reviewing the signs of active labor with her. The nurse was also asked to schedule Gillian to return for a non-stress test in 3 days unless her labor began in the interim.
